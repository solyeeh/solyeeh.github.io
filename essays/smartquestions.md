## Why ask smarter?

Anyone that is interested or has done any sort of programming will have seen or heard of the site "Stack Overflow" at least once. The website has a huge community of programmers that range from students to professional programmers. With the amount of questions being asked everyday, how can you give yourself the best chance of getting a reply? 


## What not to do

An example of a bad way of asking a question would have to be this [example](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/77057432/automate-form-filling-with-data-from-another-tab). The author wants to make an automated form filling for their job but they do not go into any more details such as what programming language they are interested in using. They ended up getting their question closed because there is no focus as the question is too broad. They also could have started on the code to show they are not lazy and just ask for help if their code doesn't compile. Instead, it makes them look like they are just asking someone else to figure out everything for them. A good rule of thumb is if you can find the answer on a google search then it's probably for the best that you don't ask the question.

## Asking smart

A [popular and good example](https://stackoverflow.com/questions/11227809/why-is-processing-a-sorted-array-faster-than-processing-an-unsorted-array) is the one where the author has a question asking why processing a sorted array is faster than processing an unsorted array. They clarified they were using C++ and that they had tried to use their code on a different language but still ended up having a similar output. They showed they put in the effort to do some research by testing it out different languages and also put a copy of both codes into the question. 


## Ask and work smarter

Programming is usually a team based activity so asking your peers for help is going to be normal. However, wasting their time by asking "stupid" questions shows that you are incompetent. Asking smart questions shows that you can clarify details and figure out exactly what problem you need help with. Asking smart questions will lead to receiving smart answers.
